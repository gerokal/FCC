Dr. Joseph Murray

Kidney transplant pionner

Here is a brief history timeline of Dr. Joseph Murray

  • 1919: He was born in Milford, Massachusetts, to William and Mary Murray².
  • 1936-1940: He attended the College of the Holy Cross and studied philosophy and English².
  • 1943: He graduated from Harvard Medical School and began his internship at the Peter Bent Brigham Hospital².
  • 1944-1947: He served in the U.S. Army Medical Corps and worked in the plastic surgery unit at Valley Forge General Hospital in Pennsylvania².
  • 1954: He performed the world's first successful renal transplant between the identical Herrick twins at the Peter Bent Brigham Hospital² .
  • 1959: He performed the first successful kidney transplant from a deceased donor³.
  • 1962: He performed the first successful kidney transplant between non-identical twins using immunosuppressive drugs³.
  • 1971: He became the chief of plastic surgery at Brigham and Women's Hospital and Children's Hospital Boston³.
  • 1981: He retired from clinical practice but continued to teach at Harvard Medical School³.
  • 1990: He shared the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with E. Donnall Thomas for their discoveries concerning organ and cell transplantation⁴.
  • 2012: He died from complications after a stroke in Boston, Massachusetts³.
